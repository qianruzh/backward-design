Here are some tips if you want to make your coffee looks good.

Make a latte art

Latte art is a method of preparing coffee created by pouring microfoam into a shot of espresso and resulting in a pattern or design on the surface of the latte.

  1. Begin with the cup tilted slightly away from you.
  2. Pour steamed milk into the center of the cup.
  3. Drop the pitcher closer to the cup; speed up your pour.
  4. Wiggle.
  5. Untilt the cup, slow down, raise the pitcher a half an inch, and finish the rosetta.

Layered dirty coffee

Dirty coffee is a doubleshot poured directly onto cold milk. When served fresh, it has a very interesting palate: the espresso on the surface and cold milk at the bottom making it hot and cold at the same time

  1. Use refrigerated ice bok.
  2. Refrigerate to cool the cup completely.
  3. Put 8 points full ice bok milk in the iced glass.
  4. Put the cup directly under the coffee machine to extract the coffee. Place the cup as close to the brewing head as possible (you may also use a spoon) to reduce the impact of the coffee liquid on the milk liquid surface when the coffee liquid drops.
